Rock Boulevard Rehabilitation Project

Civil Engineering Capstone Design — University of Nevada, Reno

Explore the Project

Purpose & Need

The Problem

Rock Boulevard in Sparks, Nevada is a critical north–south transportation corridor connecting industrial, commercial, and residential areas near the Interstate 80 interchange. The segment between Hymer Avenue and Victorian Avenue passes beneath a Union Pacific Railroad bridge where the roadway profile creates a low point that is highly susceptible to stormwater ponding and roadway flooding.

During heavy rain events, this underpass frequently experiences closures, unsafe driving conditions, and traffic congestion. Two documented flooding events in July and August 2025 resulted in complete closure of the underpass, requiring emergency response and traffic rerouting.

Why This Project Matters

  • Recurring Flooding: Stormwater runoff accumulates at the underpass low point faster than the existing drainage system can convey it away
  • Traffic Safety: Constrained lane widths, tight turning radii, and substandard geometry contribute to congestion and vehicle conflicts
  • Infrastructure Reliability: Flooding-related closures reduce network reliability and increase travel delays for commuters, freight carriers, and emergency vehicles
  • Economic Impact: The corridor serves nearby industrial and commercial areas that depend on Rock Boulevard for freight access and employee commuting

Project vicinity — Rock Boulevard near the I-80 interchange, Sparks, NV

Scope of Design

The project evaluates engineering solutions to improve stormwater performance, roadway safety, and long-term corridor reliability. Three design alternatives were analyzed, with Alternative 1: Drainage and Road Reconstruction selected as the preferred solution.

Existing Conditions Assessment

Site reconnaissance, documentation of roadway geometry, flooding conditions, drainage infrastructure, and pedestrian facilities. Collection of as-built drawings, traffic records, and historical flooding data.

Hydraulic & Hydrologic Analysis

Stormwater runoff evaluation using regional rainfall data (NOAA Atlas 14), drainage system capacity analysis, pump sizing calculations, and permeable pavement effectiveness assessment.

Structural Design

Design of a new 20-ft cast-in-place reinforced concrete cantilever retaining wall to replace the existing angled wall, enabling roadway widening and improved drainage control.

Transportation Design

Roadway regrading, lane configuration improvements, turning radius enhancements for WB-67 freight vehicles, ADA-compliant sidewalks, and traffic signal modernization.

Geotechnical Evaluation

Subsurface soil analysis, pavement section design, foundation requirements for retaining wall construction, and evaluation of excavation conditions.

Cost Analysis

Engineer's cost estimate for construction, comparative evaluation of alternatives, and life-cycle cost considerations. Total estimated project cost: $1.65 million.

Engineering Concepts & Skills Applied

Structural Engineering

Rankine Active Earth Pressure Theory for lateral loading analysis. Cantilever retaining wall design including stability checks for sliding, overturning, and bearing capacity. Reinforced concrete design per ACI 318 with shear key and drainage provisions.

Water Resources & Hydrology

Rational Method for peak runoff estimation. Manning's Equation for storm drain capacity evaluation. Pump system sizing and performance analysis. Water Quality Volume (WQv) compliance verification per NDOT methodology. Multi-event storm analysis (25, 50, and 100-year).

Transportation Engineering

Stopping Sight Distance (SSD) verification per AASHTO Green Book. WB-67 design vehicle turning analysis. Lane configuration and intersection geometry optimization. Traffic signal timing and modernization. ADA-compliant pedestrian facility design.

Geotechnical Engineering

Soil classification using USCS and AASHTO systems. Web Soil Survey data interpretation. Foundation design for retaining wall systems. Evaluation of alluvial deposits (sands, silts, gravels) for construction suitability and groundwater considerations.

Environmental & Sustainability

Stormwater quality treatment and erosion control. Permeable asphalt design for infiltration and runoff reduction. Best Management Practices (BMPs) for construction and long-term operations. Sustainability evaluation across economic, social, and environmental metrics.

Project Management & Cost Analysis

Multi-alternative feasibility comparison using cost, constructability, safety, and performance criteria. Engineer's cost estimation with itemized quantities. Project scheduling and milestone planning for Innovation Day presentation.

Community Impact

The Rock Boulevard Rehabilitation Project directly addresses the safety, mobility, and economic needs of the Sparks community. By resolving the recurring flooding and improving roadway infrastructure, this project delivers meaningful benefits to the people and businesses that depend on this corridor every day.

Cost-Effective Solution

Key Benefits to the Community

  • Reduced Flood Risk: Upgraded drainage, permeable pavement, and improved grading prevent the underpass from flooding during storms
  • Safer Roads: Wider lanes, improved turning radii, modern traffic signals, and better signage reduce crash risk for all users
  • Reliable Access: Eliminating flood closures ensures consistent access for commuters, freight operators, and emergency vehicles
  • Pedestrian Safety: ADA-compliant sidewalks and improved pedestrian facilities enhance accessibility and safety
  • Economic Resilience: Reliable infrastructure supports local businesses, industrial operations, and the regional freight network
  • Long-Term Sustainability: Permeable asphalt and stormwater BMPs improve environmental performance and reduce long-term maintenance
